I am a 3rd-year PhD student in Computer Science at Purdue University, advised by Dr. Majid Kazemian. With over 5 years of experience in computational biology, I develop machine learning and deep learning methods (including SVMs, MLPs, GNNs, VAEs, Transformers, LLMs, and foundation models) for genetics, gene regulation, single-cell/multi-omics analysis, and viral-associated diseases. I collaborate with the Olson Lab and Afzali Lab, and my PhD is supported by the Lynn Fellowship.

Research Interests

My goal is to advance machine learning models that interpret biological systems with human-level intuition and mechanistic insight. I develop scalable computational approaches for genetics, gene regulation, and single-cell biology—including large-scale analysis of single-cell transcriptomic data for interpretable gene programs; deep learning models for DNA/RNA sequence-to-function prediction; and integration of genetic variation, regulatory signals, and ML-derived representations to uncover causal drivers of disease. (See my CV for full list of publications and ongoing projects.)
